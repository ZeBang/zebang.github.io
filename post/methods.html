(3) Evaluation

How we evaluate the accuracy?

Knowing concepts: class imbalance, 4 kinds of errors (confusion matrix), Precision, Recall, F1 score.

Consider a classification model that separates email into two categories: "spam" or "not spam." If you raise the classification threshold, what will happen to precision?

Probably decrease. In general, raising the classification threshold reduces false positives, thus raising precision.

How we choose the threshold of probability?

ROC (receiver operating characteristic curve).

If we pick a random positive and a random negative, what's the probability my model ranks them in the correct order?

AUC (area under ROC curve).

What is prediction bias and does zero bias means perfect?

average of predictions == average of observations. Zero bias along does not mean everything in your system is perfect (use mean as predictions is a zero bias method but not good).

How we detect prediction bias?

Calibration plot.

How should we fix bias?

It may be because of : incomplete feature set; buggy pipeline; biased training sample; Overly strong regularization; noisy data set. Don't fix bias with a calibration layer, fix it in the model. For example, look for bias in slices of data.

Name the test for inclusion of a term and exclusion of a term.

Rao score test/Wald test.
