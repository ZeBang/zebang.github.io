                                                                2.Atoms

                                                                All data is ultimately built from atoms. An atom is an irreducible value of a specific data type.

                                                                It is a "feature" of q that the data type of an atom is negative whereas the type of a simple list is positive.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)type 42
                                                                -7h
                                                                q)type 10 20 30
                                                                7h

                                                                5.Dictionaries

                                                                A dictionary is a mapping defined by an explicit association between a key list and value list.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)`a`b`c!100 200 300

                                                                This will effectively cause the dictionary to be a hash table with the attendant improvement in lookup speed over the default linear lookup.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)(`u#`a`b`c)!10 20 30

                                                                non-unique keys are tolerated but lookup only sees the first occurrence.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)ddup:`a`b`a`c!10 20 30 20
                                                                q)ddup[`a]
                                                                10

                                                                Operations on Dictionaries

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)d:`a`b`c!10 20 30
                                                                q)d[`b]:42 / update
                                                                q)d[`x]:100 / insert
                                                                q)`a`b _ d / removing
                                                                c| 30
                                                                x| 100
                                                                q)`a`c cut d / removing
                                                                c| 30
                                                                x| 100

                                                                Following shows how to merge dictionaries

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)d1:`a`b`c!10 20 30
                                                                q)d2:`x`y!40 50
                                                                q)d1,d2
                                                                a| 10
                                                                b| 20
                                                                c| 30
                                                                x| 40
                                                                y| 50
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)d1:`a`b`c!10 20 30
                                                                q)d2:`a`b`c!100 200 300
                                                                q)d1,d2
                                                                a| 100
                                                                b| 200
                                                                c| 300
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)d1:`a`b`c!10 0N 30
                                                                q)d2:`b`c`d!200 0N 400
                                                                q)d1^d2
                                                                a| 10
                                                                b| 200
                                                                c| 30
                                                                d| 400

                                                                6.Functions

                                                                Function Specification

                                                                The symbol : will immediately terminate function evaluation and returns the value of the expression to its right. This style is discouraged.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q){[x] :x*x} / unnecessary use of :
                                                                q){[x] a:1; :x*x; b:3} / dead code

                                                                Three implicit positional parameters x, y and z are automatically defined and available in a function’s expressions when no parameters are explicitly declared.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)g:{x+z}  / likely meant x+y; requires 3 args in call
                                                                q)g[1;2]   / still waiting for 3rd arg – i.e., a projection
                                                                {x+z}[1;2]
                                                                q)g[1;2;3] / 2nd arg is required but ignored
                                                                4

                                                                functions are also data. A function can be passed around just like a long or float – e.g., as an item in a list.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)(1; 98.6; {x*x})
                                                                1
                                                                98.6
                                                                {x*x}
                                                                q)f:{x*x}
                                                                q)(f; neg)
                                                                {x*x}
                                                                -:
                                                                q)(f; neg)[0]
                                                                {x*x}
                                                                q)(f; neg)[1; 5]
                                                                -5
                                                                

                                                                q)apply:{x y}
                                                                q)sq:{x*x}
                                                                q)apply[sq; 5]
                                                                25

                                                                A variable that is assigned with : within a function body is called a local variable. For example, a is a local variable in the following function.

                                                                42
                                                                q)b
                                                                7
                                                                

                                                                q)b:6
                                                                q)f:{b:42; b::x; b}

                                                                98
                                                                q)b
                                                                6
                                                                

                                                                

                                                                q)a:42
                                                                q)f:{a:98.6; `a set x}
                                                                q)f 43
                                                                `a
                                                                q)a
                                                                43

                                                                Projection

                                                                Projection means specifying only some of the parameters in function application, resulting in a function of the remaining parameters.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)g:add[42;]

                                                                45
                                                                

                                                                q)-[;42] 98
                                                                56
                                                                

                                                                q){x+y+z}[;;3][1;] 2
                                                                6
                                                                q){x+y+z}[1;;][;3] 2
                                                                6

                                                                Apply functions

                                                                "Apply" is actually a higher-order function that takes a function and value(s) and evaluates the function at the value(s). 

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)L:(10 20 30; 40 50)
                                                                q)@[L; 1]
                                                                20
                                                                q)@[count; L]
                                                                2
                                                                

                                                                q)L . 1 0
                                                                40
                                                                

                                                                q)g:{x+y}
                                                                q)g . 1 2
                                                                3

                                                                The general form of functional @ and .

                                                                @[L; I; g; v]

                                                                .[L; I; g; v]

                                                                L can be any data structure. Here I is a top-level sub-domain of L; g is a binary function; and v is an atom or list conforming to I.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)L:10 20 30 40 
                                                                q)@[L; 0 2; neg]
                                                                -10 20 -30 40 
                                                                q)@[`L; 0 2; neg]
                                                                `L
                                                                q)L
                                                                -10 20 -30 40 
                                                                

                                                                q)L:10 20 30 40
                                                                q)@[L; 0 1; +; 100 200]
                                                                110 220 30 40
                                                                

                                                                q)@[L; 0 1; +; 100 200]
                                                                110 220 30 40
                                                                

                                                                q)@[L; 0 2; :; 42 43]
                                                                42 20 43 40
                                                                

                                                                q)m:(10 20 30; 100 200 300)
                                                                q).[m; 0 1; +; 1]
                                                                10 21 30
                                                                100 200 300
                                                                q).[m; (::; 1); +; 1 2]
                                                                10  21  30 
                                                                100 202 300
                                                                

                                                                q)@[m; (::; 1); +; 1 2] /NOTE! this is not the same as .[m; (::; 1); +; 1 2]

                                                                103 203 303

                                                                Enumerating Symbols

                                                                The process of converting a list of symbols to the equivalent list of indices described in the previous section is called enumeration in q.

                                                                Under the covers, $ does the indexing operation in the previous section and then replaces each symbol with its index.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)`u$v
                                                                `u$`msft`aapl`aapl`ibm`ibm`aapl`g`g`g`ibm`g`msft`msft`aapl`msft..
                                                                q)ev:`u$v
                                                                q)`int$ev
                                                                2 1 1 3 3 1 0 0 0 3 0 2 2 1 2 3 1 0 1 1 2 1 2 0 2 1 1 0 1 1 3 ..

                                                                We continue with the example of the previous section, renamed to use the standard sym domain.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)sym:`g`aapl`msft`ibm
                                                                q)v:1000000?sym
                                                                q)ev:`sym$v

                                                                The enumerated ev can be substituted for the original v in nearly all situations.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)v[3]
                                                                `aapl
                                                                q)ev[3]
                                                                `u$`aapl
                                                                

                                                                q)v[3]:`ibm
                                                                q)ev[3]:`ibm
                                                                

                                                                q)v=`ibm
                                                                000100010010011101000010010100000000100100000001000000001100001001011..
                                                                q)ev=`ibm
                                                                000100010010011101000010010100000000100100000001000000001100001001011..
                                                                

                                                                q)where v=`aapl
                                                                4 5 19 20 21 31 33 34 41 42 43 49 58 59 61 74 81 83 90 94 95 98 114..
                                                                q)where ev=`aapl
                                                                4 5 19 20 21 31 33 34 41 42 43 49 58 59 61 74 81 83 90 94 95 98 114..
                                                                

                                                                q)v?`aapl
                                                                4
                                                                q)ev?`aapl
                                                                4
                                                                

                                                                q)v in `ibm`aapl
                                                                000111010010011101011110010100010110100101110001010000001111011001011..
                                                                q)ev in `ibm`aapl
                                                                000111010010011101011110010100010110100101110001010000001111011001011..

                                                                Dynamically Appending to an Enumeration Domain.

                                                                One situation in which an enumeration is more complicated than working with the denormalized data is when you want to add a new value.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)sym:`g`aapl`msft`ibm
                                                                q)v:1000000?sym
                                                                q)ev:`sym$v
                                                                q)v,:`twtr
                                                                q)ev,:`twtr
                                                                'cast

                                                                The new value must first be added to the unique list.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)sym,:`twtr
                                                                q)ev,:`twtr

                                                                The syntax of ? is the same as the enumeration overload of $ – i.e., the name of a (unique) list of symbols as left operand and a source symbol or list of symbols as right operand.

                                                                This application of ? has the side effect of first checking to see if the source symbols are in the domain named by the left operand, and appends any that aren't. In any case, it returns the enumerated version of the source just like $.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)sym:()
                                                                q)`sym$`g
                                                                'cast
                                                                q)`sym?`g
                                                                `sym$`g
                                                                q)sym
                                                                ,`g
                                                                q)`sym?`ibm`aapl
                                                                `sym$`ibm`aapl
                                                                q)sym
                                                                `g`ibm`aapl
                                                                q)`sym?`g`msft
                                                                `sym$`g`msft
                                                                q)sym
                                                                `g`ibm`aapl`msft

                                                                `t`t2

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)([] name:`symbol$(); iq:`int$())

                                                                Keyed Table

                                                                A keyed table is not a table

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)v:flip `name`iq!(`Dent`Beeblebrox`Prefect;98 42 126)
                                                                q)k:flip (enlist `eid)!enlist 1001 1002 1003
                                                                q)kt:k!v
                                                                q)kt
                                                                eid | name       iq 
                                                                ----| --------------
                                                                1001| Dent       98 
                                                                1002| Beeblebrox 42 
                                                                1003| Prefect    126
                                                                q)kt:([eid:1001 1002 1003]
                                                                      name:`Dent`Beeblebrox`Prefect; iq:98 42 126)
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)ktempty:([eid:()] name:(); iq:())
                                                                q)ktempty:([eid:`int$()] `symbol$name:(); iq:`int$())
                                                                q)ktempty:([eid:0#0] name:0#`; iq:0#0)

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)([] eid:1001 1002)#kt
                                                                eid | name       iq
                                                                ----| -------------
                                                                1001| Dent       98
                                                                1002| Beeblebrox 42
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)key kt
                                                                q)value kt
                                                                q)cols kt

                                                                q)t:([] eid:1001 1002 1003; name:`Dent`Beeblebrox`Prefect; iq:98 42 126)

                                                                -------------------

                                                                1002 Beeblebrox 42 
                                                                1003 Prefect    126

                                                                9.Queries: q-sql

                                                                The first important difference is that a q table has ordered rows and columns. This is particularly useful when dealing with the situation where records arrive in time order.

                                                                A second difference is that a q table is stored physically as a collection of column lists.

                                                                A third difference is that q-sql provides upsert semantics.

                                                                select, where, fby, grouping

                                                                The select template has the following form, where elements enclosed in matching angle brackets <...> are optional.

                                                                select <ps> <by pb> from t <where pw>

                                                                The select and from keywords are required; omission or mistyping either results in an error. The table expression t, which is any q expression whose value is a table or keyed table, is also required. The remaining elements ps, pb and pw are optional. They are called the Select, the By and the Where phrases, respectively. The order of evaluation of the select template is:

                                                                1. From expression t
                                                                2. Where phrase pw
                                                                3. By phrase pb
                                                                4. Select phrase ps
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)select distinct from ([] c1:`a`b`a; c2:10 20 10)
                                                                q)select[2] from s where city<>`athens
                                                                q)select[-1] from s where city<>`athens
                                                                q)select[1 2] from s where city<>`athens
                                                                q)select[>name] from s where city<>`athens / descending 
                                                                q)select[<city] from s where city<>`athens / ascending 
                                                                q)select[2; >name] from s where city<>`athens
                                                                

                                                                q)tbig:100#t
                                                                q)select from tbig where i within 50 99 / The Virtual Column i in where
                                                                q)select from t where c2>15,c3<3.0 / Multiple Where
                                                                q)select from t where c1 within 00:00:01.000 00:00:01.999, c2=`a
                                                                / Nested Columns in Where
                                                                q)t:([] f:1.1 2.2 3.3; s:("abc";enlist "d";"ef"))
                                                                q)select from t where s like "ef"

                                                                Used in a Where phrase, fby takes the form

                                                                (fagg;exprcol) fby c

                                                                The left operand is a two-item list comprising an aggregate function fagg and a column expression exprcol on which the function will be applied. The right operand c is the column to be grouped.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)select from p where weight=(max;weight) fby city,color=`blue
                                                                p | name  color weight city
                                                                --| -----------------------
                                                                p3| screw blue  17     rome

                                                                MSFT N  12:45:00 23.45

                                                                exec

                                                                The syntax of the exec template is identical to that of select.

                                                                exec <ps> <by pb> from texp <where pw>

                                                                Whereas select always returns a table, the result type of exec depends on the number of columns in its Select phrase. One column yields a list; more than one column yields a dictionary.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)t:([] name:`a`b`c`d`e; state:`NY`FL`OH`NY`HI)
                                                                q)select name, distinct state from t
                                                                'length
                                                                q)exec name, distinct state from t
                                                                name | `a`b`c`d`e
                                                                state| `NY`FL`OH`HI
                                                                

                                                                q)exec name from t where state in `NY`HI
                                                                `a`d`e

                                                                Functional Form

                                                                Here are two functional forms, one for select and exec, the other for update and delete. The types of the arguments passed determine the overload. The forms are,

                                                                ?[t;c;b;a] / select and exec

                                                                ![t;c;b;a] / update and delete

                                                                where

                                                                t is a table or the name of a table a is a dictionary of aggregates b is a dictionary of groupbys or a flag controlling other aspects of the query c is a list of constraints. The expressions in a, b and c can involve columns of t and also any variables that are in scope. The rules for expression interpretation are:

                                                                Columns are always represented by their symbolic names Consequently, any literal symbols, or lists of symbols, appearing in the expressions must be distinguished. This is done by enlisting them.

                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)select from t
                                                                q)?[t; (); 0b; ()]
                                                                

                                                                q)select from t where c2>35,c1 in `b`c
                                                                q)?[t; ((>;`c2;35); (in;`c1;enlist `b`c)); 0b; ()]
                                                                

                                                                q)select max c2, c2 wavg c3 from t
                                                                q)?[t; (); 0b; `maxc2`wtavg!((max;`c2); (wavg;`c2;`c3))]
                                                                

                                                                q)select by c1 from t
                                                                q)?[t; (); (enlist `c1)!enlist `c1; ()]
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)select max c2, c2 wavg c3 by c1 from t where c2>35,c1 in `b`c
                                                                

                                                                q)c:((>;`c2;35); (in;`c1;enlist `b`c))
                                                                q)b:(enlist `c1)!enlist `c1
                                                                q)a:`maxc2`wtavg!((max;`c2); (wavg;`c2;`c3))
                                                                q)?[t;c;b;a]
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)t:([] c1:`a`b`a`c`b`c; c2:1 1 1 2 2 2; c3:10 20 30 40 50 60)
                                                                q)select distinct c1,c2 from t
                                                                q)?[t; (); 1b; `c1`c2!`c1`c2]
                                                                

                                                                q)t:([] c1:`a`b`c; c2:10 20 30)
                                                                q)select[2] from t
                                                                q)?[t;();0b;();2]
                                                                

                                                                q)t:([] c1:`a`b`c`a; c2:10 20 30 40)
                                                                q)select[>c1] c1,c2 from t
                                                                q)?[t;();0b;`c1`c2!`c1`c2; 0W; (>:;`c1)]
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)t:([] c1:`a`b`c`a; c2:10 20 30 40; c3:1.1 2.2 3.3 4.4)
                                                                q)exec distinct c1 from t
                                                                q)?[t; (); (); (distinct; `c1)]
                                                                q)exec c1:distinct c1 from t
                                                                q)?[t; (); (); (enlist `c1)!enlist (distinct; `c1)]
                                                                

                                                                q)exec distinct c1, c2 from t
                                                                q)?[t; (); (); `c1`c2!((distinct; `c1); `c2)]
                                                                

                                                                q)exec c2 by c1 from t
                                                                q)?[t; (); `c1; `c2]
                                                                xxxxxxxxxx
                                                                q)t:([] c1:`a`b`c`a`b; c2:10 20 30 40 50)
                                                                q)update c2:100 from t where c1=`a
                                                                

                                                                q)c:enlist (=;`c1;enlist `a)
                                                                q)b:0b
                                                                q)a:(enlist `c2)!enlist 100
                                                                q)![t;c;b;a]
