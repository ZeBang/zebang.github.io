Convergence Rate

Claim: Suppose M is a p×p symmetric matrix of rank r≤p, and let M=UΛU′ be its spectral decomposition, where U is a p×r ortho-normal matrix and Λ is a r×r diagonal matrix. Suppose M^n is a sequence of random matrices and {an} is a sequence of diverging positive numbers such that anvec(M^n−M)⇒N(0,Θ). Let M^n=U^nΛ^nU^n′ be spectral decomposition of M^n. Then U^−U=Op(1/an) and U′(U^−U)+(U^−U)′U=op(1/an).

Proof: U^−U=Op(1/an) is obvious. The claim follows by

U′(U^−U)+(U^−U)′U=−(U^−U)′(U^−U)+U^′U^−U′U,

since U^ and U are both ortho-normal matrices.
